FAQ

Common questions answered
What is the difference between GI and GP pipes?
GI and GP pipes are both galvanized steel pipes, but differ in how and when the zinc coating is applied ‚Äî affecting thickness, durability, and ideal use cases.

GI Pipes (Galvanized Iron Pipes)

  • Made from iron or steel, coated with a thick layer of zinc via hot-dip galvanization ‚Äî submerging in molten zinc
  • Thick coating offers superior corrosion resistance and durability in harsh environments
  • Common uses: water supply systems, structural support in buildings & bridges, oil & gas industry

GP Pipes (Galvanized Pipes)

  • Made from pre-galvanized steel sheets ‚Äî zinc coating applied before the pipe is formed
  • Generally thinner zinc layer; lighter in weight for easier handling and installation
  • Common uses: fencing, scaffolding, agricultural irrigation systems
